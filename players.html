Legendary World Cup Teams

Since the creation of the beautiful tournament, there have been a few national teams who have stood out from others for their success, style of play, and impact on the game. These countries have changed the way soccer is played and have set standards for teamwork, creating global icons. These teams have had an impact worldwide to the point where people recognize their colors, jerseys, and players without even supporting them.

Some of these countries include Brazil, Italy, Germany, Argentina, and France and are recognized for their World Cup success. The teams are filled with top players and they have some of the most watched matches in the World Cup. The table showcases the top national teams in the World Cup and the number of trophies they have won.

World Cup Legends

The World Cup has set the stage for some of the greatest players in soccer history. They are known for their top performances in the tournament which has cemented them as some of the World Cup's all time greats. Learn more about how the tournament grew over time on the History page.

Iconic World Cup Players

These players are remembered not only for their skills, but also for their performance at the highest possible level when pressure was most intense. Their goals, assists, skills, and leadership qualities have helped them win trophies for their countries and inspired millions worldwide.

  • Pelé (Brazil): Pelé is regarded by many people as the best player of all time. He is the only player to win three World Cups (1958, 1962, 1970) and became a world known symbol of Brazilian football.
  • Diego Maradona (Argentina): Known for flair and creativity, he led Argentina to World Cup victory in 1986. He is remembered for his controversial "Hand of God" and "Goal of the Century" in the same tournament.
  • Lionel Messi (Argentina): Messi led Argentina to victory in 2022 after many years in his career chasing the trophy. Widely considered the greatest of all time, his performance in the Qatar 2022 World Cup completed one of the most successful careers ever in soccer history.
  • Kylian Mbappé (France): Mbappé, a young and modern superstar, helped France win the 2018 World Cup in Russia at only 19 years old. He became the second-youngest player to score in a World Cup final after Pelé and managed to score a hat-trick in the 2022 final. He showed the world that a new generation of players are ready to take on the future of soccer.
